## 2. Instantly — Best All-in-One Platform

**Starting at $37/month (Growth plan)**

Instantly has grown from a simple sending tool into an entire cold email ecosystem. They now offer a lead database (450M+ contacts), email verification, CRM, and even done-for-you email account provisioning — all alongside their core sending functionality.

### What Instantly Does Well

- **Massive feature set:** Lead database, email verification, warmup, A/B testing, and AI-powered features all in one
- **450M+ B2B contacts database** (SuperSearch) — find leads without leaving the platform
- **Unlimited email accounts** on all plans
- **Email account provisioning** — buy domains and set up email accounts via API
- **Audit logs and compliance features** — important for agencies

### What Instantly Gets Wrong

- **Shared infrastructure** — your emails share IPs with everyone else on the platform
- **Pricing adds up fast** — $37 for sending + $97 for leads + $47 for CRM = $181/mo for the full stack
- **5,000 email limit on base plan** — need Hypergrowth ($97/mo) for serious volume
- **Complex pricing tiers** — features are gated behind higher plans (A/B testing, webhooks require $97+)

**Pricing:** $37/mo (5K emails) → $97/mo (100K) → $358/mo (500K)

**Verdict:** Instantly is the most feature-rich option, but you pay for it. Great for teams that want everything in one place and don't mind shared infrastructure. If deliverability is your priority, pair Instantly with ColdRelay for dedicated infrastructure.

## 3. Smartlead — Best for Agencies

**Starting at $39/month (Basic plan)**

Smartlead has carved out a strong position in the agency market with client-level accounts, unlimited email accounts, and competitive pricing at scale.

### What Smartlead Does Well

- **AI ESP matching** — automatically matches senders to leads based on email provider for better deliverability
- **Campaign-level webhooks** — granular webhook control (not just workspace-level)
- **Simple pricing** — $379/mo gets you 500K emails with everything included
- **Unlimited email accounts** on all plans
- **Good agency features** — client-level filtering and management

### What Smartlead Gets Wrong

- **Low API rate limits** — 5 requests/2 seconds (300/min). Painful for automation.
- **No email verification** — need a separate tool
- **Shared infrastructure** — same IP reputation problem as Instantly
- **Can't delete campaigns** — only archive. Minor but annoying.
- **No unified inbox API** — can't programmatically manage replies

**Pricing:** $39/mo (6K emails) → $94/mo (150K) → $379/mo (500K)

**Verdict:** Best bang-for-buck at high volume. If you're an agency sending 500K+/month across client accounts, Smartlead's flat $379/mo is hard to beat. Just use ColdRelay for the infrastructure layer.

## The Infrastructure Question

You can use the best cold email software in the world, but if your emails are sent from shared IPs with terrible senders, you'll land in spam.

1. **ColdRelay** for infrastructure (dedicated domains, IPs, DNS, warmup)

## FAQ

### What is the best cold email software for beginners?
Instantly or Mailshake. Both have intuitive UIs and built-in warmup. Start with Instantly's $37/mo Growth plan.

### What is the cheapest cold email software?
Saleshandy at $25/mo for 10,000 emails. But cheap software on shared infrastructure = poor deliverability. Invest in infrastructure (ColdRelay) first.

### Do I need separate infrastructure for cold email?
Yes. Sending cold email from Google Workspace or shared platforms puts your domain reputation at risk. Dedicated infrastructure (like ColdRelay) isolates your sending reputation.

### How many emails can I send per day with cold email software?
Depends on your infrastructure. With dedicated IPs and properly warmed accounts, 200-500 per mailbox per day is safe. With shared infrastructure, keep it under 50 per mailbox.

### Which cold email software has the best deliverability?
Deliverability depends on infrastructure, not software. Any tool connected to ColdRelay's dedicated infrastructure achieves 95%+ inbox placement. On shared infrastructure, results vary widely.
