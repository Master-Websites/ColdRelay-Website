## What Are Email Finder Tools?

Email finder tools help you discover professional email addresses for specific people or companies. They work by:

- Crawling the web for publicly available email addresses
- Using email pattern detection (firstname@company.com)
- Verifying addresses via SMTP checks
- Enriching data from business databases and social profiles
- Matching LinkedIn profiles to email addresses

## Email Finding Best Practices

1. **Always verify before sending** — finding an email isn't the same as confirming it's valid. Run found emails through a verifier before adding to campaigns.

2. **Use multiple tools** — no single tool has 100% coverage. Layer 2-3 tools for maximum find rate.

3. **Check the email pattern first** — if you know the company uses firstname@company.com, you don't need a tool for every contact.

4. **Respect opt-outs** — if someone asks to be removed, remove them immediately regardless of how you found their email.

5. **Don't scrape personal emails** — focus on business email addresses. Finding someone's personal Gmail for cold outreach is invasive and often illegal.

## FAQ

### How accurate are email finder tools?
Top tools achieve 85-95% accuracy for verified results. Unverified guesses (pattern-based) are less reliable. Always verify before sending.

### Is it legal to find and email someone's business address?
In the US (CAN-SPAM), yes — with an unsubscribe link and physical address. In the EU (GDPR), you need legitimate business interest. B2B email outreach is generally legal with proper compliance.

### Can I find personal email addresses with these tools?
Some tools return personal emails, but you shouldn't use them for cold outreach. Focus on business emails — it's more professional and legally safer.

### How many emails should I find per day?
Depends on your outreach capacity. If you can send 50 emails/day, finding 50-100/day (accounting for invalid addresses and unsubscribes) is reasonable.

### Why do different tools give different emails for the same person?
Tools use different databases, algorithms, and sources. One might find their current company email while another returns an old address. Cross-reference multiple tools for the most accurate result.
