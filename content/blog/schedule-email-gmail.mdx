## How to Schedule an Email in Gmail (Desktop)

### Step-by-Step

1. **Open Gmail** and click **Compose** to start a new email
2. **Write your email** as you normally would (recipients, subject, body)
3. Instead of clicking "Send," click the **small arrow** next to the Send button
4. Select **"Schedule send"**
5. Choose one of the suggested times, or click **"Pick date & time"** to set a custom schedule
6. Select your preferred **date and time**
7. Click **"Schedule send"**

That's it. Your email will be sent automatically at the scheduled time.

### Where to Find Scheduled Emails

After scheduling, your email moves to the **Scheduled** folder in the left sidebar. You can:
- **View** all pending scheduled emails
- **Cancel** the scheduled send (the email moves back to Drafts)
- **Edit** the email (cancel first, make changes, reschedule)

## How to Schedule an Email in Gmail (Mobile)

### iPhone / iOS

1. Open the **Gmail app**
2. Tap **Compose** (the pencil icon)
3. Write your email
4. Tap the **three dots** (⋮) in the top right corner
5. Select **"Schedule send"**
6. Pick a suggested time or tap **"Pick date & time"**
7. Set your preferred date and time
8. Tap **"Schedule send"**

### Android

1. Open the **Gmail app**
2. Tap **Compose**
3. Write your email
4. Tap the **three dots** (⋮) in the top right
5. Tap **"Schedule send"**
6. Choose a suggested time or **"Pick date & time"**
7. Set your date and time
8. Tap **"Schedule send"**

The process is nearly identical on both platforms.

## How to Edit or Cancel a Scheduled Email

### On Desktop
1. Go to the **Scheduled** folder in the left sidebar
2. Open the email you want to change
3. Click **"Cancel send"** at the top of the email
4. The email moves to your **Drafts** folder
5. Edit as needed, then either send immediately or reschedule

### On Mobile
1. Tap the **hamburger menu** (☰) → **Scheduled**
2. Open the scheduled email
3. Tap **"Cancel send"**
4. Find the email in Drafts, edit, and reschedule

**Important:** You can't edit a scheduled email in place. You always have to cancel, edit, and reschedule. It's a two-step process.

## Gmail's Suggested Send Times

When you click "Schedule send," Gmail suggests three times:

- **Tomorrow morning** (typically 8:00 AM)
- **Tomorrow afternoon** (typically 1:00 PM)
- **Monday morning** (if it's a weekend — 8:00 AM Monday)

These are based on generic best practices, not personalized data. For optimal timing, use the custom "Pick date & time" option.

## Best Times to Schedule Emails

Timing affects whether your email gets opened or buried. Here's what the data says:

### General Business Emails
| Day | Best Time | Why |
|-----|-----------|-----|
| Tuesday | 10:00 AM | Inbox is cleared from Monday rush |
| Wednesday | 8:00 AM | Caught during morning routine |
| Thursday | 2:00 PM | After-lunch email check |

### Cold Outreach Emails
| Day | Best Time | Why |
|-----|-----------|-----|
| Tuesday | 8:00-9:00 AM | Top of inbox when they start work |
| Thursday | 7:00-8:00 AM | Before inbox gets busy |
| Avoid | Friday PM, weekends | Low open rates |

### Follow-Up Emails
| Day | Best Time | Why |
|-----|-----------|-----|
| 3 days after first email | Same time as original | Consistency in their routine |
| Tuesday or Thursday | 10:00 AM | Peak engagement windows |

**Key insight:** Schedule emails to arrive in the recipient's timezone, not yours. An email scheduled for 9 AM your time might arrive at 6 AM theirs — and get buried by the time they check.

## Gmail Schedule Send Limits

Gmail has some restrictions:

- **Maximum scheduled time:** Up to 49 years in the future (effectively unlimited)
- **Minimum scheduled time:** At least 2 minutes from now
- **Number of scheduled emails:** No official limit, but Gmail may slow down if you have hundreds pending
- **Timezone:** Uses your Gmail account's timezone setting

### Changing Your Gmail Timezone
If your scheduled sends are going out at the wrong time:
1. Go to **Gmail Settings** → **General**
2. Find **"Time zone"** (or it inherits from your Google Account)
3. Go to **Google Account** → **Personal info** → scroll to **"Other info"**
4. Update your timezone

## Why Schedule Emails Instead of Sending Immediately?

### 1. Reach People at the Right Time
An email sent at 11 PM gets buried under the morning flood. An email arriving at 9 AM sits at the top of the inbox.

### 2. Respect Work-Life Boundaries
Sending emails at midnight signals that you expect after-hours responses. Scheduling for business hours is more professional and considerate.

### 3. Batch Your Email Work
Write all your emails at once during a focused block, then schedule them throughout the week. This is more efficient than context-switching between tasks and emails all day.

### 4. Account for Time Zones
Working with international clients or remote teams? Schedule emails to arrive during their working hours, not yours.

### 5. Never Miss a Follow-Up
Write the follow-up email at the same time as the original, and schedule it for 3-5 days later. If they reply before then, cancel the follow-up. If they don't, it goes out automatically.

## Troubleshooting

### Scheduled email didn't send
- Check the **Scheduled** folder — it might still be there
- Verify your internet connection was active at the scheduled time (Gmail sends from Google's servers, so this shouldn't matter — but check to confirm the email isn't stuck in Outbox)
- Make sure you didn't accidentally cancel it

### Wrong timezone
- Update your Google Account timezone (Account → Personal info → Other info)
- After changing, new scheduled emails will use the updated timezone
- Already-scheduled emails use the timezone that was set when you scheduled them

### Can't find the Schedule Send option
- Make sure you're using the latest version of the Gmail app
- On desktop, the option is in the dropdown arrow next to the Send button
- On mobile, it's in the three-dot menu (⋮) — not always obvious

### Schedule send not available in your organization
Some Google Workspace admins disable this feature. Contact your IT department if you don't see the option.

## FAQ

### Can I schedule a Gmail email to send in a different timezone?
Not directly. Gmail uses your account's timezone. To send at 9 AM in someone else's timezone, you'll need to calculate the offset and schedule accordingly. For example, if you're in EST and they're in PST, schedule for 12 PM your time to hit 9 AM theirs.

### Can I schedule recurring emails in Gmail?
No. Gmail's built-in scheduler only supports one-time sends. For recurring emails, you'll need a third-party tool like Boomerang, or set calendar reminders to send them manually.

### Does scheduling an email affect deliverability?
No. Scheduled emails are sent from Google's servers just like regular emails. There's no deliverability difference between an immediately sent and a scheduled email within Gmail.

### Can I schedule an email with attachments?
Yes. Add attachments before scheduling — they'll be included when the email sends. Just make sure the attachment is fully uploaded before you click "Schedule send."

### What happens if I'm offline when a scheduled email is supposed to send?
It sends anyway. Gmail's scheduled sends are handled by Google's servers, not your device. Once scheduled, it sends regardless of whether you're online.

### How many emails can I schedule at once in Gmail?
There's no official limit on the number of pending scheduled emails. However, you're still subject to Gmail's daily sending limits (500/day for free accounts, 2,000/day for Workspace).
