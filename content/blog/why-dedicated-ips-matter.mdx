# Why Dedicated IPs Matter for Cold Email at Scale

If you're running cold email at any meaningful volume, the infrastructure underneath your campaigns matters more than your copy, your targeting, or your sending schedule. And at the foundation of that infrastructure sits one critical decision: **dedicated vs. shared IPs**.

## The Shared IP Problem

When you use Google Workspace, Outlook, or most third-party resellers for cold email, you're sharing IP addresses with thousands — sometimes millions — of other senders. Your emails ride on the same IPs as everyone else's.

This means:

- **One bad actor tanks everyone.** If someone sharing your IP sends spam, your reputation drops too.
- **You have zero control.** You can't warm up a shared IP. You can't manage its reputation. You're along for the ride.
- **Inconsistent deliverability.** Your inbox placement can fluctuate wildly based on what other senders on your IP are doing.

## Why Dedicated IPs Change Everything

With dedicated IPs, your sending reputation is entirely your own. Here's what that means in practice:

### 1. Your reputation is yours alone

No one else's behavior affects your deliverability. Send well, maintain good list hygiene, and your reputation stays clean.

### 2. Predictable deliverability

Without the noise of shared infrastructure, your inbox placement becomes predictable and consistent. You can actually optimize because you control all the variables.

### 3. Scale without fear

On shared IPs, scaling volume often means hitting more spam filters. With dedicated IPs, scaling means building stronger reputation through consistent, well-managed sending.
